Overview

WiSense is an Android based platform being developed by the WiNGS Lab at the University of Wisconsin-Madison for wireless debugging and diagnosis in both enterprise and home WLANs. The platform allows users to analyze the channel quality, monitor the signal quality of neighboring WLANs and detect the presence of neighboring non-WiFi activity. The ease of usage and mobility provided by handheld device platforms can allow network administrators as well as users to identify location specific wireless performance issues in their WLANs. This tool is also being developed as part of our group's efforts to collect data about wireless performance at different locations and share it with the community.

So, in brief, WiSense lets you:

  • Analyze wireless activity on different channels.
  • Detect neighboring non-WiFi activity.
  • Monitor airtime utilization across different WiFi channels.
  • Scan neighboring access SSIDs..

Features

Following is the breakdown of different capabilities provided by WiSense-

Spectrum Heat Map

Using WiSense, users can analyze heat map of the spectrum for different channels in real-time through a waterfall display.


Events

Users can also see what all non-Wifi devices, interfering with their WiFi, have been detected by the application. WiSense also provides other details about the detected devices such as their non-WiFi signal strengths, theri operating frequency bandwidth and channel information. Apart from the current real-time activity, application keeps track of eariler detected activity as well.


Non-WiFi Detected Devices

Users can also see what all non-Wifi devices, interfering with their WiFi, have been detected by the application. WiSense also provides other details about the detected devices such as their non-WiFi signal strengths, theri operating frequency bandwidth and channel information. Apart from the current real-time activity, application keeps track of eariler detected activity as well.


SSID

WiSense also captures neighboring SSID information and the SSID to which device is currently connected. It shows you the different wireless activity in the vicinity of the device.
